# 1a. Research Objective

## Research Brief

Money Monsters is a gamified financial literacy app for young people — styled after Duolingo — designed to help users learn to "tame their money monsters" through progressive lessons on budgeting, saving, interest, and investing. With launch imminent, this research was commissioned to establish product–market fit and identify clear pre-launch priorities.

The central question: *not just "is it usable?" but "will they choose it, stick with it, and learn from it?"*

Six decision-oriented objectives framed the work:

| # | Area | Objective |
| - | ---- | --------- |
| 1 | Desirability | Does the proposition land instantly — do users get what it is, who it's for, and why they'd choose it over alternatives? |
| 2 | Triggers & barriers | What prompts teens to try or return — and what causes drop-off or inertia? |
| 3 | Usability & comprehension | Can users navigate confidently and understand content without help? Where do they stumble? |
| 4 | Engagement & motivation | Does the gamification create momentum? What drives progress — and what causes boredom or abandonment? |
| 5 | Pre-launch priorities | What needs fixing now vs. queued post-launch, based on impact on clarity, engagement, and learning? |
| 6 | Premium & future direction | What content and features could drive retention and justify a premium tier? |

---

## Approach

- Expert review: financial literacy landscape analysis; surfacing current approaches and learning mechanisms
- Develop AI synthetic personas to develop user need statements, proposition directions
- Fieldwork: conducted via Zoom

- **60-minute screen-share and think-aloud sessions** — participants navigated the app on their own device while the moderator observed and probed in real time. Discussion structure followed:
	- Attitudes and behaviors towards money
	- Current and desired knowledge of money topics
	- Money needs exercise: Ranking centered statements to establish key motivators around money education
	- Proposition exploration: exploring 3 different directions
	- Prototype: user-driven exploration of home screen, onboarding and 2 sample lessons

---

## Participant Overview

- **UK:** 19 participants across three age segments
- **Czechia:** 10 participants, same segment split

- Recruited to be mainstream — not finance enthusiasts
- Deliberately skewed toward lower financial literacy and lower household income
- Excluded: finance-industry households and anyone with formal financial training
- Most had minimal or no useful financial education at school

| Segment           | n (UK) | Ages  | Profile                                                             |
| ----------------- | ------ | ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Young Teens (YT)  | 6      | 12–15 | Pocket money; no financial independence                             |
| Older Teens (OT)  | 6      | 16–18 | First earnings; approaching key life decisions                      |
| Young Adults (YA) | 6      | 18–25 | Early employment or higher education; real financial responsibility |
